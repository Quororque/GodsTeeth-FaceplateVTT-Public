How did you cope?

I almost gave up when I realized we were never supposed to have been there in the first place. There wasn't any hope of an official cover-up or smudging-over, and there still isn't. I didn't feel safe at home, for whatever safe was going to mean. I spent weeks and then months imagining my fingerprints being lifted, hair follicles collected, warrants drafted. I waited to see a bearcat in my driveway. There was too much drinking to do in too little time. It makes memory incoherent, any pain becomes duller, and for a while I was desperately pursuing the idea that if I poisoned myself severely enough, often enough, it might kill anything I didn't throw up on the floor of that fucking cottage.

What is the new normal?

It was almost impossible to focus on work, but I wasn't known for being outspoken in the first place, and nobody seemed to notice when I never had much to add to a conversation. I was obsessed with the idea that they were spying on me, setting me up: That they're going to be the ones who testify against me in court, against my character, and about how I'm a dangerous person. I knew they had me in a vice - why give up a good paycheck? I figured some of it would find it's way back to my family if it all went bad, in the end. It never did. I fell up and into being an independent case agent, and then a pretty fucking senior one. My clearance was reinvestigated and renewed without a hitch. My friends don't understand any of this or how it's all connected. To me, it's crystal clear: There's a real, smoldering kind of evil in the world. It's the Devil, but every people have a different name for it. I thought I could find some kind of solace in talking to them, at least in the things we still did share, but my beliefs and some drunk confrontations pushed them far, far back. I think a lot about God in the Old Testament. Judges is my favorite book, now. They don't get it.

Where were you on 9/11?

A fishing trip with the kids was aborted after we heard about it on the radio. I was convinced that this was "it", the big something, that the dominos were starting to fall and a lot of people were about to start wishing they spent a lot more time reading the Bible. When it was finally over, I couldn't stop thinking about the people who jumped. I watched it over and over again, inside my head if it wasn't on the TV screen. Why didn't I do the same thing in Maryland? When you're staring down certain death, maybe it really could be called going on your own terms. I didn't have too much time to think about any of that shit - there was plenty of work chasing down every criminal under the sun and their half-assed attempts to recreate a bomb worthy of a newspaper headline.

What did it take to recruit you?

Very little. I think if they'd come to my front door instead of waiting at the gas station down the road, I might've done something stupid. They were frank, and had probably talked to enough people around me to know they didn't need to say much. They know that I know that I'm trapped, that I could be wrapped up at any moment, but if I'm valuable to them... I can keep moving. My family will be safe. America remains.
