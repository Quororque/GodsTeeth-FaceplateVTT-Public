The Long Years: 2001-2016

It is August 25, 2016. The world is on fire. We know many things about the long years. We know the choices that the Teeth make in the aftershocks of the night at Cornucopia House. We know how they respond to further exposure to Delta Green. When Delta Green’s newly official “Program” comes recruiting in the early or mid-2000s, the Teeth individually agree to join, unaware that the others have done the same. They sign on with various case officers. Perhaps in their own ways they each mask guilt with enthusiasm. They may have seen joining the Program as a chance to get at Agent Clove and the people behind Cornucopia House. It soon becomes clear that the War on Terror leaves Delta Green little time to investigate Clove’s extracurricular activities. Nor does anyone in the Program know to push for it. The names of the Teeth were found in the back of some filing cabinet, referring to their compartmentalized friendly status. The details of their histories were lost in the bureaucratic shuffle. The officers who recruit them do not refer to the details of unnatural incidents the Teeth might already have experienced. Nor do they ask for any. They are extraordinarily enthusiastic about getting experienced people 'on board' to fill in the growing need for more operatives. All the better that they may be veterans to the mind-shattering unnatural. Those first case officers soon are cycled off to other assignments. Their replacements know better than to ask details of prior operations without first clearing it with unsympathetic and suspicious supervisors. Better to leave the past alone. The Teeth make themselves useful in the Program but keep their heads down. The dread drains away with the years. There may be time to heal, to rationalize, to forget, but not to escape. (edited)

The Survivors

The Long Years is a story written by the players themselves.
  • How did you cope?
Try to get some idea of your characters home life as they come back from their ordeal. Do they obsessively watch the news, seeking clues about the abandoned kids? Do they flee town in a panic and try to obscure their identities? Do they quietly sob in the shower, crawl into bed, and mumble something about a late shift to a spouse before staring into the shadows of the ceiling?
  • What is the new normal?
Nothing in your characters entire life approaches the madness of that night at Cornucopia House in early 2001, which makes the memory of it stick out all the more. What is the new normal for your character? Where do they live? Who do they work for? At this point in time, your character has (or may have) worked for the federal government for decades. Promotions are inevitable. Describe how your mundane life went on to snowball into something larger.
  • Where were you on 9/11?
The 9/11 attacks changed everything. Your character will forever remember where they were when they heard the news. They can narrate how their jobs changed, or how the expansion of the security state led to promotion and advancement. But does it even get close to what happened that night?
  • What did it take to recruit you?
For everyone, that night at Cornucopia House was the last contact they had with the old, fractured Delta Green conspiracy. However, sometime in the 2000s, each of you are approached by a recruiter working for the official "Program". They don’t identify themselves with the name Delta Green more than once or twice, but it’s clear they got the character’s name from somewhere before. The recruiter doesn’t know about Clove or anything that happened in Maryland. How does the Program approach you? Most importantly for the campaign to continue, why did you say yes? (edited)

  • How did you make yourself useful?
Much of the work your character did for the Program is alone. Every month or two, they are contacted by a lone officer using a false name. Your character investigates rumors to see whether a supposed incident is actually worth investigation by a team. You occasionally receive calls for help, and most of those turn out to be completely bogus, and the ones that matter are handed off to someone else while you are sent home. Without knowing they are assets of a far deeper conspiracy than any depth official intelligence goes, they may assume they are being treated as second-rate. How does your character approach this work? How do they respond to it? Describe your slow march towards obscurity.
  • Choose three (3x at once) home pursuits!
You must pick three individual (may repeat) home pursuits from Page 78 of Agent's Handbook and detail them. You may also imagine your own pursuit outside of these ones, it will likely make sense. Typically, “home” scenes occur between missions and reflect months or even years of time. They are a chance to explore the most important things in an Agent’s life, and to see whether those things are spiraling down. Remember: combat skills are limited by your occupation and cannot be trained further unless you have a significant change of career. Crafting a characters entire personal life in these short scenes differs from the more procedural elements of play. It might call for large time skips and parallel narratives. Keep this tip in mind. Limitations

Below are non-negotiable limitations to your (back)story that you are required to follow. Do not try to worm around them or come up with a justification as to why it's 'possible', for it is not.
  • The Teeth do not die.
  • The Teeth do not receive injuries or criminal charges that make them unfit for further service. They do not lose eligibility to acquire and maintain a security clearance. It may be deactivated due to a career change, but it is never revoked.
  • The Teeth do not change their identities.
  • The Teeth do not contact anyone involved in Cornucopia House or return to Baltimore, MD or Cecilton County, MD.
  • The Teeth do not recount the events of Cornucopia House to anyone. Whatever their exposure to such horror results in, it does not leave the confines of their mind and/or create evidence against them.
  • The Teeth do not go bankrupt or achieve extreme financial success.
  • The Teeth, if went abroad, are back to a domestic posting by January 1, 2016 at the latest. They remain within the United States after this cutoff date. Likewise, the cutoff date for home pursuits is the same.
  • The Teeth do not come in contact with the genuine unnatural. Either with or without Delta Green, their life has returned to mundanity after the events of Cornucopia House. If they did, it's bogus.
  • The Teeth cannot use stay on case action.
