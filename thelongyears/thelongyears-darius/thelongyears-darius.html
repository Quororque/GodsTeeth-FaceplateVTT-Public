How did you cope?

How would anyone go about coping with such an encounter? The events just kept replaying in my mind, every night plagued with some frantic recollection to myself of what I’d seen. It wasn’t long until the notion of a good night’s rest became some distant fantasy, I couldn’t tell you how many more hours I’ve spent gazing at the ceiling than actually sleeping. I think the only thing stopping me from losing it was the fact I knew whatever I saw was gone. That, and the sleeping pills I’ve had to start taking.

What is the new normal?

I returned home from my trip in some vain attempt to act like nothing had happened. When it came to it, I was half prepared to walk into work, already cuffed for what had went down that night. Deciding against an immediate confession, clocking into work was almost uncanny in how run of the mill it was. You’d think, with such fear hanging over me, working on cases would be at least a little different. Work, and life itself, were characterised by a constant paranoia. I’d killed people. Evil people, without a doubt, but people enough to get me in serious shit. I’m sure that cop had someone looking out for him. Turns out you can’t just “get used” to such a fear, every day just as taxing as the last.
