Maryland gun laws (early 2001)
  • Pistols: Handgun purchases require a Maryland State Police background check and a 7-day waiting period. Buyers have to complete a Maryland State Police Application to Purchase a Regulated Firearm. Certain small pistols are banned under "Saturday Night Special" roster of 1996. Carrying a handgun requires a Maryland Wear & Carry Permit.
  • Long guns: No waiting period for standard rifles/shotguns. Standard instant federal red-flag background check (NICS) required. Certain regulated models require State Police Application & 7-day waiting period.
  • Ammunition: Unregulated.
  • Body armor: Unregulated.
Availability (to law enforcement level): modular carriers: rare trauma (plate) vests: uncommon soft kevlar vests: common flak vests: everywhere modular kevlar helmets: rare outdated kevlar helmets: common steel helmets: everywhere riot gear: common for LE, rare for civilians
  • Accessories: Unregulated. Peak of tech: optics - holographic, NVGs - Gen 3 ($unusual to $extreme depending on # of tubes)
  • NFA Items: Suppressors, machine guns registered pre-1986, SBRs, etc. Subject to same federal restrictions (ATF Form 4, Form 1, CLEO sign-off, tax stamp, huge waiting time).
  • Destructive devices: Explosives, pyrotechnics, other chemical weapons: Illegal without nigh-impossible to obtain federal licensing for individuals.
---
  • Melee Weapons: Unregulated. Legal to own, except Maryland has a stature for "dangerous weapons and intent" which prosecutes carrying switchblades, telescopic batons and brass knuckles for civilians.
  • Less-than-lethal: OC sprays, tear gas sprays: unregulated. Tazers: unregulated but banned in Baltimore. Beanbags, pepperball, teargas, flashbangs, stingballs: LE only.
