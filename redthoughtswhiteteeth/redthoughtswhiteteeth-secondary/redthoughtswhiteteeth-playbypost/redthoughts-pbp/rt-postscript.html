Maryland gun laws (2016)
  • Pistols: Handgun purchases require a Maryland State Police background check and a 7-day waiting period. Buyers must complete the Maryland State Police Application to Purchase a Regulated Firearm. Maryland enforces an approved handgun roster (successor to the earlier “Saturday Night Special” concept), meaning only listed handguns can be sold. Additionally, under the 2013 Firearm Safety Act, buyers must obtain a Handgun Qualification License (HQL) before purchasing a handgun (fingerprints, background check, safety training required, with limited exemptions). Carrying a handgun requires a Maryland Wear & Carry Permit, issued on a restrictive “good and substantial reason” basis.
  • Long guns: No waiting period for standard rifles/shotguns. Standard instant federal red-flag background check (NICS) required. Certain regulated models require State Police Application & 7-day waiting period.
  • Regulated firearms (post-2013 changes): Maryland bans many so-called “assault weapons” (e.g., AR-15 pattern rifles in many configurations).
  • Ammunition: Unregulated.
  • Body armor: Unregulated except for ex-convicts.
Availability (to law enforcement level): heavy modular carriers: uncommon modular carriers: common soft kevlar vests: common modular kevlar helmets: uncommon outdated kevlar helmets: common riot gear: common for LE, rare for civilians Unlisted armor: depends on private purchases, usually available.
  • Accessories: Unregulated. Peak of tech: LPVOs, NVGs - Gen 3 ($unusual to $extreme depending on # of tubes)
  • NFA Items: Suppressors, machine guns registered pre-1986, SBRs, etc. Subject to same federal restrictions (ATF Form 4, Form 1, CLEO notification under ATF 41F, tax stamp, huge waiting time).
  • Destructive devices: Explosives, pyrotechnics, other chemical weapons: Illegal without nigh-impossible to obtain federal licensing for individuals.
