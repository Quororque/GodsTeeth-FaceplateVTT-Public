Florida gun laws (2016)
  • Pistols: Handgun purchases require a background check through the state FDLE. 3-day waiting period on handgun purchases unless the buyer possessed a Florida Concealed Weapon or Firearm License (CWFL) or qualified for another exemption. No handgun roster, permit-to-purchase system, or registration. Carrying a handgun concealed requires a Florida CWFL, issued on a shall-issue basis. Open carry generally prohibited except during certain outdoor activities (hunting, fishing, camping, etc.).
  • Long guns: No waiting period for ordinary rifles or shotguns under state law in most areas. Standard dealer background check required. No registration. No state assault weapon ban.
  • Regulated firearms: Florida had no general state-level assault weapon ban or handgun roster in 2016.
  • Ammunition: Mostly unregulated. Armor-piercing handgun ammunition, exploding ammunition, dragon’s breath shotgun shells, bolo shells, and flechette shotgun shells prohibited.
  • Body armor: Generally unregulated for ordinary possession. Illegal to wear body armor during commission or attempted commission of certain felonies.
Availability (to law enforcement level): heavy modular carriers: uncommon modular carriers: common soft kevlar vests: common modular kevlar helmets: uncommon outdated kevlar helmets: common riot gear: common for LE, uncommon for civilians Unlisted armor: depends on private purchases, usually available.
  • Accessories: Mostly unregulated. Peak of tech: LPVOs, NVGs - Gen 3 ($unusual to $extreme depending on # of tubes)
  • NFA Items: Suppressors, machine guns registered pre-1986, SBRs, SBSs, etc. Subject to federal restrictions (ATF Form 4, Form 1, CLEO notification under ATF 41F, tax stamp, long waiting time).
